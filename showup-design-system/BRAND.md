# ShowUp — BRAND.md

## The Mark

The ShowUp logo is an exercise in structured dissent — a geometric symbol that communicates both corporate-grade precision and underground urgency. The mark is built from two identical solid vertical rectangles of equal height, connected by a single thin horizontal line positioned near their tops. This is the abstracted scale: justice, balance, equilibrium of power. The vertical elements carry weight; the horizontal connector is a line of precision.

At first glance, the mark reads as a technical symbol — something from a manual, a schematic, or a tactical diagram. On second glance, it resolves into its namesake: the scales. This dual reading is intentional. It signals that the tool is both infrastructure and ideology.

The geometry is uncompromisingly rectilinear. No curves. No decorative flourishes. The proportions are derived from a strict grid, ensuring that the mark feels engineered rather than drawn. The vertical rectangles should feel dense and grounded; the horizontal line should feel sharp and exact.

This sharpness is intentional and absolute — the logo mark is the one place in the entire system where the edge does not soften. In a world of pill-shaped buttons and rounded cards, the ShowUp mark remains a blade. This tension between the approachable interface and the uncompromising identity is the brand's signature: you are welcomed by the tool, but the cause is never rounded.

## Variations

**Symbol + Wordmark (Full Lockup)**
Used for launch screens, marketing materials, and any context where brand recognition matters most. The symbol sits to the left of the wordmark "SHOWUP" set in Space Grotesk Bold, all caps. The spacing between symbol and wordmark is exactly equal to the width of the horizontal connector line.

**Symbol Only**
Used for the app icon, favicon, and any context smaller than 48px in height. At these scales, the wordmark becomes illegible and the symbol must carry the entire identity. The symbol-only variant is the primary mark for the product itself.

**Wordmark Only**
Used sparingly — primarily in text-heavy contexts where the symbol has already been established. Headlines, document headers, or watermark applications.

**Monochrome**
The mark works in solid white on black, solid black on white, or any single color from the neutral palette. The monochrome variant is the default for most UI applications.

**Reversed**
White mark on dark backgrounds. This is the dominant treatment in the product, where dark surfaces are the rule.

## Clear Space

The mark always has room to breathe — a margin equivalent to the height of one vertical rectangle, on all four sides. This is not generous; it is necessary. The mark loses its authority when crowded. In the full lockup, the clear space is measured from the outermost element (symbol or wordmark).

## Scale Thresholds

At the smallest sizes — a 16px favicon, a toolbar icon — only the symbol survives. The wordmark disappears. At this scale, the mark must still read as intentional, not as an accident. The two vertical rectangles and connecting line should remain distinct down to 16px. Below this threshold, the mark is replaced by a single solid circle (the "emergency" fallback).

At large scales — hero backgrounds, signage — the mark can be used at any size, but the line weights should never be scaled beyond 4px stroke weight. Instead, the mark is composed of solid shapes that scale proportionally.

## Color Usage

**On Dark Backgrounds (Default)**
The mark is rendered in solid white (#F0F0F0) or in the atomic orange accent (#FF4D00) for moments of emphasis — splash screens, empty states, or primary CTAs.

**On Light Backgrounds**
The mark is rendered in solid black (#050505) or in the dark charcoal (#141414). Atomic orange is permitted only when the mark is the primary focal point of the composition.

**On Brand-Color Backgrounds**
When placed on the atomic orange surface, the mark must be rendered in solid black (#050505) or very dark charcoal (#0A0A0A) to maintain maximum contrast and visual weight.

## What the Logo Must Never Do

- **Never be stretched, squeezed, or distorted in any axis.** The proportions are fixed. The vertical rectangles must always be identical in width and height.
- **Never be placed on a visually competing background without a protected zone.** No photos of crowds, no busy textures, no gradients behind the mark.
- **Never be rendered in an off-brand or unapproved color.** No neon green, no pastel pink, no corporate blue. The palette is strict: black, white, grays, atomic orange, and the approved semantic colors.
- **Never be combined with another brand's mark without an explicit lockup rule.** No co-branding without design review.
- **Never be rotated or flipped.** The scale reads upright. Rotation destroys its meaning and makes it appear accidental.
- **Never be used at a scale where it becomes illegible or loses its character.** The symbol-only mark has a minimum size of 16px. The full lockup has a minimum size of 32px height.
- **Never have a drop shadow, glow, or outline added.** The mark lives flat against its surface. Depth is achieved through contrast, not effects.
- **Never be rounded or softened.** While the interface uses generous rounding, the mark remains rectilinear. Adding curves to the logo destroys the tension that defines the brand.
